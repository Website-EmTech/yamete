Climate Change in Baguio City

According to the World Wide Fund for Nature-Philippines, Baguio City is the most vulnerable to climate change among the four cities outside the National Capital Region because of its susceptibility to extreme weather conditions. The study found that Baguio City, the nation's summer capital, is most vulnerable to the consequences of climate change, with rain being its worst adversary. Intense storms might cut off Baguio City's economic activity due to floods and landslides. The fast urbanization and high population density, would make it more difficult for the city to adapt to the changes.

Causes

CO2 produced by human activities is the largest contributor to climate change, as well as land pollution,and water pollution

Effects

*Shortage of water in green spaces
*Disaster and climate change
*Tropical cyclones (floods,landslides,earthquake and wildfires)
*High elevations

Solutions

*Reduce,reuse,repair,recycle
*save energy at home
*Switch to sustainable transport
*Restore nature to absorb more carbon
*Protect the oceans
*Protect forest like the Amazon
and more...
